What are the Sustainable Development Goals?

The Sustainable Development Goals, also known as Global Goals are a group of 17 interconnected goals created to acheive "peace and proesperity for the people and the planet, now and in the future."

They are an effort to protect the planet, end poverty and inequality and ensure that all people enjoy health, justice and prosperity.

These are the three of the 17 sustainable development goals
(You can hover on them to learn more about each goal):

SDG 4

The full title of SDG-4 is "Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning oppurtunities for all."

SDG 16

The mission statement for the 16th SDG is to "promote peacefull and inclusive societites for sustainable development, provide access to justive for all and build effective, accountable and inclusive institutions at all levels."

SDG 17

The official mission statement for the 17th SDG is "strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development".
